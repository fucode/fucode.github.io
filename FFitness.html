Summary

There’s so much to improve in the cloud storage industry. The user experience is competitive in both the consumer and business marketplace. Ferrari Fitness wants to give Personal Trainers the cloud storage tools to provide their clients round the clock fitness programs from wherever they are in the world.

Problem

Clients want something that caters to there needs in the emerging cloud storage space. Fitness professionals have problems with the current cloud storage technologies because they do not focus on the niche market of the fitness industry.

  • Current cloud technologies can get cluttered
  • The app is caters to Trainers to make their jobs easier
  • There is no niche cloud solution dedicated to Personal Trainers
  • Trainers want to be able to coach clients that are mobile

    • Solution

    Ferrari fitness allows Trainers to design fitness programs for clients no matter where they travel in the world.

  • The fitness cloud storage is a lot less cluttered than other competitors
  • The app is caters to Trainers to make their jobs easier
  • Trainers and Clients can focus on just their fitness regimen and not have to deal with other files in the cloud
  • Trainers are able to provide an alternative to clients who can not afford 1-on-1 training Linke to

    Hi-Fi Mockups & Testing

    Maze Testing

    During Hi-Fi testing we found 72.7% of users were able to use the prototype with ease. So we felt comfortable in moving foreware with our design.

    During testing users had trouble finding the sign up button on the original design because it was below the fold. We adjusted that and added some additional changes for our users. 86.7% users prefered the finalized landing page over the original design.

    Users felt the original pictures describing the product did not match the theme of the site. 92.7% of users prefered the new pictures.

    The footer of the landing page was adjusted after user feedback stating it was not appealing and hard to read. 96.8% of users prefered the new pictures.

    During testing users also described how they wanted to see client stories of using the product. So we added it in the final design.

    Conclusion

    What worked?

  • User Testing provided much needed feedback on what to change going foreward.
  • Using Figma vs. Sketch as a design tool if working across different OS
  • Maze is a great way to do testing to get metrics for design.
    • Doubts going into the project?

  • The cloud storage space is saturated with many competitors
  • Will users like our design?

    • What didn’t work?

  • Trying to work with Sketch when you have to work across different OS systems for design. For the sake of time we had to ensure the design team could work across various devices.
  • Assuming that our designs were good and we did’nt need testing.

    • Suprises

  • Users had a very easy time navigating the prototype
  • Taking feedback for the logo actualy made it better.

    • What I learned? In the future...

  • Test early and often.
  • Use Figma if working across various OS systems.
  • Ensure something is readable to others users. Something may seem easy to read for the designer, but after testing we can see what is the truth.
