Background: ServiceNow

ServiceNow is a leading cloud-based platform that streamlines enterprise operations through IT Service Management (ITSM), employee workflows, and customer service solutions. It empowers organizations to automate processes, enhance productivity, and deliver seamless user experiences.

  • Unified Platform: Integrates IT, HR, customer service, and custom applications on a single platform.
  • Automation-Driven: Leverages AI and low-code tools to automate repetitive tasks and workflows.
  • User-Centric Design: Provides intuitive interfaces for employees, IT agents, and customers.
  • Scalable Architecture: Supports enterprises of all sizes with cloud-native scalability.
  • Global Adoption: Used by thousands of organizations worldwide across industries.

Key Challenges

ServiceNow operates in a competitive enterprise software market, facing challenges in meeting diverse user needs, ensuring seamless integrations, and maintaining platform performance at scale.

  • Complex User Needs: Balancing customization for diverse industries while maintaining simplicity.
  • Integration Gaps: Ensuring seamless connectivity with third-party tools like Salesforce or SAP.
  • Adoption Barriers: Overcoming resistance to change among users transitioning to new workflows.
  • Performance at Scale: Maintaining speed and reliability as user and data volumes grow.
  • Cost Perception: Addressing concerns about platform costs relative to perceived value.

Action Plan for a New ServiceNow Product Owner

As a new Product Owner for ServiceNow’s core platform, a structured action plan is critical to align with stakeholders, streamline processes, and drive platform enhancements. The following outlines key actions to understand incoming work, build relationships, facilitate design sessions, and optimize documentation using generative AI.

  • Understand Incoming Work: Review backlog in Jira to assess feature requests, bugs, and technical debt; conduct 1:1s with stakeholders (IT, HR, business units) to clarify priorities and align on platform goals.
  • Build Relationships: Establish trust through regular check-ins with developers, UX designers, and end-users via Slack and in-person meetings; host informal coffee chats to understand team dynamics and user pain points.
  • Facilitate Design Sessions: Lead collaborative workshops using Miro boards to ideate on features like AI-driven automation; ensure cross-functional input from engineers, designers, and stakeholders to align on user stories and prototypes.
  • Understand Discussions: Actively participate in sprint planning and retrospectives; take detailed notes during technical and business meetings to grasp platform challenges, using Confluence to summarize key points for reference.
  • Product Meeting Minutes: Document action items, decisions, and follow-ups from product meetings in Confluence; share concise summaries via email to ensure transparency and accountability across teams.
  • Manage Documentation: Audit existing documentation in ServiceNow’s Knowledge Management and Confluence to identify gaps; create standardized templates for user guides, release notes, and technical specs to ensure clarity.
  • Address Duplicative Efforts: Identify overlapping documentation or redundant workflows (e.g., multiple ticketing guides) through stakeholder interviews and analytics; consolidate into single, authoritative sources in ServiceNow.
  • Consolidate Processes: Streamline backlog grooming and feature prioritization by integrating stakeholder feedback loops via Qualtrics; use ServiceNow’s Flow Designer to automate repetitive documentation tasks.
  • If Possible Leverage Generative AI: Utilize ServiceNow’s AI capabilities, such as Now Assist, to auto-generate meeting summaries, draft user guides, and suggest knowledge base articles; train teams on AI tools to reduce manual documentation effort by an estimated 30%.

By executing this action plan, the Product Owner can accelerate platform improvements, foster collaboration, and deliver a user-centric ServiceNow experience that drives enterprise efficiency.

Design Review Board (DRB) Engagement Plan

The Design Review Board (DRB) plays a key role in ensuring ServiceNow platform solutions align with enterprise standards, architectural best practices, and user experience principles. As a Product Owner, engaging with the DRB early and consistently drives long-term scalability and compliance.

  • Understand the DRB Process: Review DRB submission requirements, evaluation criteria, and meeting cadence; familiarize yourself with approval workflows and documentation templates.
  • Prepare Artifacts: Develop wireframes, process flows, and data models in advance of reviews using tools like Lucidchart or Miro; ensure accessibility and UX standards are addressed.
  • Schedule Reviews Early: Align with project timelines to engage the DRB early in the design phase; avoid delays caused by last-minute changes or misaligned architecture.
  • Track DRB Decisions: Maintain a DRB log in Confluence with meeting dates, reviewers, decisions, and follow-ups; share updates with developers and stakeholders to ensure changes are incorporated.
  • Promote a Feedback Culture: Use DRB feedback constructively to drive continuous improvement; collaborate with engineers and UX to address concerns before final implementation.

By integrating with the DRB process, the Product Owner ensures platform enhancements are secure, scalable, and strategically aligned—ultimately improving quality and reducing rework.

API Management Strategy for ServiceNow

Robust API (Application Programming Interface) management is critical to ensure ServiceNow integrates securely and efficiently with enterprise systems. As a Product Owner, driving a clear API strategy helps enable automation, real-time data access, and improved cross-platform workflows.

  • Inventory Existing APIs: Work with developers and platform architects to audit currently available ServiceNow REST (Representational State Transfer) and SOAP (Simple Object Access Protocol) APIs; identify gaps, deprecated endpoints, and high-usage areas.
  • Define API Use Cases: Collaborate with business units and technical teams to gather integration requirements—such as HRIS (Human Resources Information Systems), IAM (Identity and Access Management), or CMDB (Configuration Management Database) automation.
  • Establish Governance Standards: Implement version control, naming conventions, and secure authentication protocols such as OAuth 2.0 (Open Authorization); ensure all external API usage complies with enterprise security and data privacy standards.
  • Monitor Performance: Use ServiceNow’s native API analytics or external tools (e.g., Splunk, Datadog) to track usage, latency, error rates, and throttling; optimize high-traffic endpoints for performance and scalability.
  • Document & Share: Create a centralized API catalog in Confluence or the ServiceNow Developer Portal with usage examples, response formats (e.g., JSON, XML), and rate limits to support self-service integration.
  • Plan for Reusability: Encourage modular, reusable API design patterns to support multiple workflows; reduce duplicate development by cataloging available endpoints across teams.

By aligning API management with business needs, compliance standards, and technical best practices, the Product Owner ensures ServiceNow remains a secure, scalable platform for enterprise-wide automation and digital transformation.

Potential Areas to Improve User Experience (UX) Improvements for Wells Fargo’s ServiceNow Platform

Enhancing the User Experience (UX) on Wells Fargo’s ServiceNow platform is critical to delivering intuitive, efficient, and engaging digital interactions for both employees and customers. By leveraging User Experience research, content-driven design, and ServiceNow’s capabilities, this section outlines actionable steps to optimize navigation, streamline workflows, and align interdisciplinary teams, ensuring a seamless end-to-end experience that supports Wells Fargo’s motto, “Together we’ll go far.”

  • Conduct User Experience Research with Stakeholders: Engage employees and customers through card-sorting and tree-testing exercises to design an intuitive Information Architecture (IA). Use surveys and interviews to create user personas, capturing needs, pain points, and ideal experiences, ensuring alignment with business objectives.
  • Optimize Navigation with Advanced Portal Navigation: Enable ServiceNow’s Advanced Portal Navigation to enhance content discoverability in the Employee Center. Organize Knowledge Management and Service Catalog content hierarchically, moving from general to specific, to simplify user journeys and reduce navigation time.
  • Enhance Search with Artificial Intelligence Search: Configure ServiceNow’s Artificial Intelligence (AI) Search to deliver relevant results by creating synonym dictionaries for Wells Fargo-specific terms and stop-word dictionaries to exclude irrelevant terms. Regularly tune search configurations to improve accuracy and relevance for users.
  • Streamline Workflows with Content-Driven Design: Collaborate with marketing, digital experience leaders, and Information Technology (IT) teams to integrate clear, concise content into workflows, avoiding technical jargon and acronyms. Use ServiceNow’s Flow Designer to automate repetitive tasks, reducing employee effort and improving process efficiency.
  • Ensure Accessibility Compliance: Design interfaces compliant with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG). Incorporate responsive design for accessibility across devices and use alternative text for images to support visually impaired users.
  • Leverage User Experience Analytics: Utilize ServiceNow’s User Experience Analytics to track user interactions and behaviors. Analyze adoption, retention, and usage Key Performance Indicators (KPIs) to identify friction points and prioritize improvements, ensuring data-driven enhancements.
  • Prototype and Iterate with Users: Develop prototypes for new features using tools like Figma or ServiceNow’s User Interface (UI) Builder. Conduct iterative testing with pilot groups of employees and customers to refine workflows and ensure alignment with user needs before full deployment.
  • Train Teams on Content and User Experience Best Practices: Provide training on creating accessible, user-friendly content and using ServiceNow’s Now Assist to auto-generate knowledge articles and summaries. Foster a “User Experience galaxy” mindset to align cross-functional teams on shared design goals.

By implementing these User Experience improvements, Wells Fargo can enhance the ServiceNow platform’s usability, accessibility, and efficiency, delivering a seamless experience for employees and customers. This approach strengthens interdisciplinary collaboration, aligns with business goals, and drives user satisfaction and adoption.
