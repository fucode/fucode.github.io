Early Computers Used by Bill Gates and Steve Jobs

Mainframe systems, microcomputers, and prototypes that shaped their early experiences.

Computer/System Year Used By Type Purpose / Role
PDP-10 (DEC) 1968 Bill Gates Mainframe Gates and Paul Allen used this at Lakeside School through a deal with CCC to learn programming (BASIC, assembly).
PDP-8 (DEC) 1970 Bill Gates Minicomputer Used in high school and early programming experiences, smaller than PDP-10 but key to learning systems.
Altair 8800 1975 Bill Gates & Paul Allen Microcomputer Inspired Gates and Allen to create BASIC interpreter and found Microsoft.
Apple I 1976 Steve Jobs & Steve Wozniak Microcomputer First product from Apple Computer; sold as a kit with keyboard and monitor support.
Apple II 1977 Steve Jobs Personal Computer First mass-market success with color graphics and expandability. Launched Apple into the mainstream.
Xerox Alto (via PARC visit) 1979 Steve Jobs Workstation Prototype Influenced the GUI of the Lisa and Macintosh after Jobs' team visited Xerox PARC.
Lisa Computer 1983 Steve Jobs (initially) Personal Computer First Apple computer with a GUI; expensive and commercially unsuccessful but influential.
IBM 7094 1960s Indirect (inspired early computing culture) Mainframe Common in universities; while not directly used, it shaped the computing environment Gates and Jobs grew into.

1987AppleMacintosh IIFirst color Mac, modular and expandable.
