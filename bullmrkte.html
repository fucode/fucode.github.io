Summary

Everyone wants to become an entertainer, athlete, win the lottery, make money through social media, or some other outlier career. Unfortunately, the stats show the vast majority of us will have to join the workforce. Demand for high skilled labor and talent across many industries continues to get harder and more challenging. It will only continue to get more difficult as the population grows, automation continues, and people live longer and retire later in their lives.

sentiment_very_dissatisfied

Problem

Companies are seeking talented individuals that are ready with the right skills to take on challenging high skilled jobs. It can be a difficult and daunting task to navigate the treacherous job market. It takes a combination of luck, hard work and persistance to land your dream job or career.

There are so many paths an individual can take to find a meaningful job or career. Do you need a degree? Should you take a boot camp? What are the costs going to be? Sometimes that path to a meaningful and fulfilling career can take a very long time or never happen at all. You will have to go from job to job and try to figure out what works best for you and your lifestyle.

sentiment_very_satisfied

Solution

Bullmrkte is a platform for students and job seekers to help guide them and motivate them to find meaningfull careers. Our platform provides industry insights and trends, career roadmaps, goal tracking for free. For paid services you can connect with an industry mentor, accountability coach, or career services to help with resumes and cover letters.

Take charge of your life and career with Bullmrkte, because no matter what type of market the economy is in, you must think like a bull for your career.

Discovery Phase

The Process of our design began with understanding the market and our potential end-users. We conducted User Surveys and Competitive Analysis to ensure our solution will satisfy our stakeholders and justify our solution. Below are some of the results from our interviews and surveys.

Survey Results

80% of professionals surveyed did not know what they wanted to do with their careers in high school or college

60% of professionals surveyed have careers that do not align with their college degree

Fitness is the most popular app in the accountability/coaching space, so that leaves room for growth in the career accountability/coaching space

When asked about payment options, 50% of users surveyed would like free services from a coaching/accountability app

Key Takeaways from Competitive Analysis


For our competitive research, we decided to focus on three goal setting and habit tracking apps that help people accomplish their goals. Bullmrkte is a platform to help students focus on their career goals.

Coach.me has brand recognition and a free habit tracker that comes with the app. The typical coaching starts off at $14.99/week.

Stickk makes you sign a contract that if you do not meet your goals you have to pay a monetary penalty to a non-profit.

Beeminder is similar to Stickk makes you sign a contract that if you do not meet your goals you have to pay a monetary penalty to a non-profit.

Competitive Summary: Comparing all the competitors we felt we had to differentiate ourselves. Bullmrkte will focus on the niche market of career roadmaps and a mentor network. We will provide free services such as career roadmaps, goal setting, and industry trends and insights to gain customers.

Low-Fi Testing

Low-Fi testing was conducted with both in-person and remote users. Users were instructed to accomplish the following tasks:

1) Onboarding for new users

2) Selecting "Career Roadmaps" and navigate to "Tech", and then "Front-End Web Development". Select a mentor and go to the payment portal to pay for a one-time session.

Conclusion

What worked?

Focusing on the MVP product of career roadmaps and mentorship opportunities first. All the additional requirements can be added in later. goal setting, accountability coaching, career services, and job trends.

Always try to create something functional as soon as possible and get it tested. Refinements can always be made in later iterations.

What didn’t work?

Using too many branding colors with the UI. Too much red was overwhelming for users eyes.

What I learned?

For the UI its is always best to try to stick with greys and whites with splashes of branding color for CAB buttons or secondary buttons.
